The Implications

With The Native Cats and Silver Fleet Ships

FRIDAY 10th DECEMBER 2021 - 7.30pm

Fresh out of the city’s devoted and supportive local scene come young Hobart natives The Implications – here to knock your socks off with a delicious set of assorted batterings, howls, plucks and grunts. Consisting of three close school friends Julian Clarke, Georgia Gillies and Mem Thorpe, The Implications’ sound is a diverse and accessible infusion of punky aggression, broken-hearted soul and primal grooves. The trio switch instruments and lead vocals between songs, bringing a wild, unpredictable energy to each track and keeping the audience on their toes – while remaining within the timeless and powerful rock set-up of electric guitar, bass and drums.

Supports
The Native Cats Indescribable (but here we go) post-punk minimalism mashed together with existential lyrics and life-or-death femininity, from a legal transcription typist and a library technician. Alluringly bleak, surprisingly danceable.

Silver Fleet Ships Connecting the dots from Spoon to Bauhaus and Queens of The Stone Age to Joy Division, Silver Fleets Ships create a dark atmosphere with intense pressure. Having just recently released their Debut EP ‘Relevator’, Silver Fleet Ships are here to fulfil all your Post-Glam Disco Bawl Sad Boi Rock n Roll wants and needs.
