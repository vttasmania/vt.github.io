About Us

Bringing the very best of live music, festivals and events to the shores of Tasmania. Vandemonian Touring has hosted world class acts including RUFUS DU SOL, The Presets, Golden Features, Vera Blue, Meg Mac, Boy & Bear, Birds of Tokyo, PNAU, and Peking Duk at Tasmania's most prestigious venues.


Also establishing some of Tasmania's premiere festivals and events, including Day on the Lawn, the Tasmanian Wine Festival, and the Huon Blues and Roots festival.

Hobart Uni Bar

One of Hobart's most iconic venues, the Hobart Uni Bar has been home to live music for over three decades. The watering hole for generations of UTAS students, the Hobart Uni Bar has existed as an accessible space for all music lovers!


Follow the Hobart Uni Bar on Facebook, or learn more at hobartunibar.com.au

The Goods Shed

The 1914 heritage-listed shed is located in the Macquarie Point precinct on Hobart’s waterfront. After recent renovations, The Goods Shed is home to some of Tasmania's best live-music gigs.


Many high profile Australian acts have graced the stages at The Goods Shed, including SAFIA, The Jungle Giants, Peking Duk and The Kite String Tangle.

Mac2

Hobart's premium waterfront venue, the former maritime port warehouse now serves both as Tasmania's cruise ship terminal and premiere events space.

Mac2 recently served as Golden Features & The Presets exclusive one night show venue.

Day on the Lawn

Combining the picturesque surrounds of the Botanical Gardens and some of Australia's best live-music acts, Day on the Lawn serves as a staple to the Tasmanian summer event calendar.

Previous headliners have included Boy & Bear, Something for Kate, Birds of Tokyo and Spiderbait.

Tasmanian Wine Festival

Discover the very best of Tasmanian wine, food and live music at the Tasmanian Wine Festival. Follow the trail through the scenic gardens to find our states best wine makers, and sit back on the grass and enjoy local and interstate live music.

Learn more at taswinefest.com.au

Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens

Established in 1818, The Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens is by far one of Hobart most unique event locations. Surrounded by historic plants and trees dating back to the 19th century, the gardens has hosted a wide variety of live-music events including Day on the Lawn, Tash Sultana & the Tasmanian Wine Festival.
